Typographics 2021 Case Study.

In Typography III, I participated in a class project that explored non-Latin Typography. I was assigned to work with a partner on the Devanagari type. The project involved developing a type system that involved both Latin and non-Latin scripts. This included creating a logo inspired by cultures who use Devanagari, sharing our guidelines with our assigned course partner and using our partner’s logo to develop a poster and landing page for the Typographics 2021 Festival.

Quick Overview

Processes and Objectives of the Project

Develop a Type System

Our instructor assigned non-Latin type to develop a latin and non-Latin type system. I was assigned Devanagari and created a type system using the fonts Lato and Palanquin.

Research and develop a Logo inspired by Devanagari

I gathered many examples of multi-script advertising and logos to create a Typographics Festival Logo that would stay true to India's culture.

Use partner's guidlines to create a poster and mobile landing page with their logo

Adobe and figma guidelines of our type system and logo's were shared with our partners to collaborate in a final logo and poster design.

Rough Draft and Revisions of the Typographics poster

Using the design brief I developed a draft of the poster. The draft was revised and corrections were made to be incorporated into the final edit.

Create a Mobile landing page in English and Devanagari

The last step of this project was to use the logo design and create a mobile landing page for the event with one in English and the other in Devanagari.

Project Objective

The objective of this project was to understand and incorporate multi-script design into the Typographics 2021 Festival. Non-Latin script is emerging quickly as a platform for designers to leverage as our world becomes more equal and diverse.
